The Duty to Consult — Key Principles3 items
▾
1
All governments must consult with and accommodate affected Aboriginal interests— established in Haida and Taku River (SCC, 2004)
2
Applies regardless of whether the claim has been PROVEN— the duty arises even if the Aboriginal group has not yet established its claim to the land/resources
3
Duty applies to GOVERNMENT only, not private parties— but consulting impacted Aboriginal groups is a PRUDENT step for private parties too, to prevent future legal action
Beyond standard EIA consultation: Environmental impact assessments (Ch.23) generally include a general consultation process with affected people, but the SCC's Aboriginal consultation obligations go BEYOND that general process.

Proven vs Unproven Title — 2 Different Obligations2 items · Tsilhqot'in 2014
▾
1
Title NOT Proven— government obligation is to CONSULT and ACCOMMODATE
2
Title PROVEN— government must obtain CONSENT before development can occur (a much higher bar than consult/accommodate)
Project planning implication: Project managers must account for the RISK OF DELAYS or design changes arising from this duty in their contracts and plans — the degree/nature of required consultation depends on the circumstances.

Nature of Reserve Property — Security Implications3 items
▾
1
Builders' liens and mortgages CANNOT be registered— reserve land is generally unpatented Crown land
2
Leases CAN be registered — but only with FEDERAL government consent— and registration occurs in a SEPARATE federal registry in Ottawa, not the usual provincial land registry
3
Traditional security forms are largely unavailable— parties doing business on reserve land must consider ALTERNATIVE forms of security
4
Aboriginal Participation in Projects — Advantages & Disadvantages2 items
▾
+
ADVANTAGE: Income tax exemption— increases potential profit margins. Joint ventures and partnerships generally PRESERVE this tax-exempt status, since profits flow directly to the individual participants. Seek specific legal advice to maintain status in group projects.
–
DISADVANTAGE: Difficulty getting bonded— stems directly from the inability to mortgage reserve lands and the difficulty collecting against on-reserve property
Practical fix: To support Aboriginal contractor participation, project owners/GCs should consider DELETING bonding requirements or providing alternatives — e.g. an INCREASED contractual holdback in lieu of bonding.
5
Contracts with Band Councils2 items
▾
1
Band councils are NOT incorporated entities— their legal status has been subject to debate
2
A band council RESOLUTION authorizes the contract— analogous to a corporate board resolution. Obtain this authorization BEFORE entering into the contract or starting work.
Contracts with band councils are generally enforceable, but seek legal advice if there is any concern about authorization or enforceability.
