2 Types of Agent Authority2 items
▾
1
Express Authority (Actual Authority)— created by contract between principal and agent; explicitly defines limits of authority
2
Apparent Authority (Ostensible / Implied Authority)— created by representations made by the PRINCIPAL to the third party (NOT by the agent's own claim). If principal knows agent is exceeding authority and does nothing, apparent authority may expand.
Key rule: An agent claiming their own authority does NOT create apparent authority. The representation must come from the principal.

Pay-if-Paid vs Pay-when-Paid — 2 Distinctions2 items
▾
1
Pay-if-Paid— condition PRECEDENT to payment; if owner never pays prime contractor, prime contractor is NOT obligated to pay subcontractor. Shifts risk of owner non-payment to subcontractor. Not always enforced — courts interpret narrowly.
2
Pay-when-Paid— affects TIMING only; prime contractor must still eventually pay subcontractor even if owner never pays. Only delays (does not eliminate) the obligation.
To enforce pay-if-paid: Must explicitly create a condition precedent AND ideally include words that the subcontractor accepts the financial risk of owner insolvency.
8
2 Types of Specifications2 items
▾
1
Performance Specification— sets out operating parameters / outcomes required (e.g. elevator speed, number of cars). Trade contractor designs and constructs. Do NOT mix detailed components into a performance spec.
2
Standard Specification— describes in detail all individual components of the final product. Designer prescribes the methodology.

▾
1
Inconsistency of terms and language
2
Repetition of material, often containing different information
3
Incorrect or inconsistent drawing notations
4
Lack of coordination between drawings, between specifications, and between drawings and specifications
5
Use of drawings/specifications without appropriate amendment— especially common with CAD and word processors
6
Incorrect use of legal jargon
7
Describing not just the required result but also mandating methodology— methodology should be left to the contractor
8
Using drawings and notes with no written specifications
