5 Types of Construction Contracts (by payment method)5 items
▾
Contract TypeHow Payment WorksKey Feature
Fixed Price / Stipulated SumLump sum including overhead and profitOwner cannot direct means/methods. Contractor keeps savings, bears overruns. Price rarely stays fixed (change orders).
Cost-PlusActual cost + fee (fixed or % of cost)No incentive to save; % fee rewards higher cost. Owner bears cost risk. Used when scope is uncertain.
Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP)Cost-plus up to a cap; savings sharedHybrid of fixed-price and cost-plus. Owner audits costs if contractor claims savings.
Unit PriceSet price per unit of work (e.g. $/m³ concrete)Common for roads, earthmoving, pipelines. Quantity adjustments may apply if totals vary significantly from estimate.
Alliance AgreementCost-plus with painshare / gainshareNo-blame clause; all parties share success/failure; no litigation allowed. Contractor's exposure limited to overhead and profit.

9 Standard Clauses Found in Most Contracts9 items
▾
a
Scope of Work— must clearly define services and materials; more accurate = fewer claims
b
Contract Time— "time is of the essence" applies to BOTH parties; schedule must be realistic with contingency time
c
Changes— must follow written change procedures; oral changes create a new contract and undermine the original
d
Damages and Bonuses— liquidated damages are enforceable; penalty clauses are NOT. No requirement to include a bonus clause alongside liquidated damages.
e
Warranty— time-limited promise to repair defects; must be clear if it is IN ADDITION TO or IN PLACE OF rights at law
f
Termination— early termination requires fundamental breach + notice + opportunity to remedy. "Termination for convenience" requires reasonable compensation.
g
Indemnification— only as good as the financial strength of the indemnifying party
h
Exclusion / Limitation / Waiver Clauses— construed strictly against the drafter; ambiguity resolved in favour of other party; 3-stage Tercon test applies
i
Dispute Resolution— notice of claim provisions must be followed strictly; mandatory arbitration vs litigation: consider carefully before including
5
3-Stage Tercon Test for Exclusion Clauses3 items · must pass all 3
▾
1
Does the exclusion clause apply to the circumstances of the case?
2
Was the exclusion clause unconscionable at the time the contract was made?
3
Should the court override the clause for public policy reasons?
Key rule: Exclusion clauses must clearly define the exact contingency to be excluded. If clearly drafted with no ambiguity, courts give full effect. Ambiguity → construed against the drafter (contra proferentem).
