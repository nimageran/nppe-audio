Standard Form Contracts
(e.g., CCDC)
Click to flip
The Basics: Pre-written templates created by industry committees.

The Advantage: They are heavily tested in court. Everyone knows exactly what the clauses mean.

The Catch: They must be modified with "Supplementary Conditions" to fit unique projects.
Pre-Tested, Not Perfect
Stipulated Price
(Fixed Price / CCDC 2)
Click to flip
The Story: The contractor agrees to build the whole building for exactly $5 Million.

The Rule: The Contractor takes all the risk. If lumber prices double, the contractor loses money. The Owner gets absolute price certainty.
Contractor Eats the Risk
Cost-Plus
(CCDC 3)
Click to flip
The Story: The scope of work is a total mystery (e.g., a messy renovation).

The Rule: The Owner pays for all actual materials/labor, plus a fee/percentage for the contractor. The Owner takes all the financial risk if the project drags on.
Owner Eats the Risk
Unit Price
(CCDC 4)
Click to flip
The Story: Road building. You don't know exactly how much dirt needs to move, but you agree to pay "$50 per cubic meter."

The Rule: The rate is fixed, but the final quantity is variable. It balances the risk between Owner and Contractor.
Fixed Rate, Unknown Total
Design-Build
(CCDC 14)
Click to flip
The Story: The Owner is tired of the Architect and Contractor blaming each other for mistakes.

The Rule: The Owner signs ONE contract with a single entity that does both the design and the construction. If something breaks, the Design-Builder is 100% responsible.
One Throat to Choke
Construction Management
(CM) Contracts
Click to flip
CM as Agent: The CM just gives advice for a fee. The Owner signs all contracts with the trades.

CM at Risk: The CM acts like a General Contractor, signs the contracts with the trades, and takes on the legal risk of finishing the job.
Advisor vs. Boss
Public-Private Partnerships
(P3 / DBFO)
Click to flip
The Story: The government needs a hospital but wants to pay for it slowly like a mortgage.

The Rule: A private company Designs, Builds, Finances, and Operates the facility for 30 years. The government just pays them a monthly lease.
Private Money, Public Asset
Supplementary Conditions
Click to flip
The Trap: You use a standard CCDC but cross out a clause you don't like.

The Rule: Modifying one clause often accidentally breaks another. If your edits create ambiguity, the court will use *Contra Proferentem* and rule against you.
Edit Standard Forms Carefully
Notice of Claim Clauses
Click to flip
The Story: You hit bad soil. You fix it, wait 3 months, and then hand the owner a bill for the extra work.

The Rule: Courts enforce notice clauses strictly. If the contract says "Must notify owner within 7 days," and you miss the deadline, you forfeit the right to get paid.
Speak Now or Lose the Cash
Excusable vs.
Compensable Delays
Click to flip
Excusable (e.g., Weather/Strike): You get extra time, but NO extra money.

Compensable (e.g., Owner changes the design): You get extra time AND extra money to cover your ongoing overhead.
Time vs. Time & Money
