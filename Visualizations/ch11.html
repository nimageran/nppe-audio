1. The Financial Risk Spectrum (Payment Models)

In construction, the method of payment fundamentally dictates who eats the cost when things go wrong. Standard forms (like CCDC documents) are built around these payment architectures.

graph LR subgraph Contractor Bears the Risk FP[Fixed-Price / Stipulated Sum] end subgraph Shared/Variable Risk UP[Unit Price] end subgraph Owner Bears the Risk CP[Cost-Plus] end FP -.-> |Contractor eats overruns, keeps savings| C1(e.g., CCDC 2) UP -.-> |Price per unit fixed, but total quantity varies| C2(e.g., CCDC 4) CP -.-> |Owner pays actual costs + overhead fee| C3(e.g., CCDC 3) style FP fill:#fee2e2,stroke:#dc2626,stroke-width:2px style UP fill:#fef3c7,stroke:#d97706,stroke-width:2px style CP fill:#e0f2fe,stroke:#0284c7,stroke-width:2px

Visualization Logic: This outlines the shift of financial burden.

  • Fixed-Price (CCDC 2): The Contractor promises to build the project for a lump sum. The myth is that this price never changes. In reality, change orders and unforeseen conditions always alter it. However, base efficiency risks sit squarely on the Contractor.
  • Unit Price (CCDC 4): Used when quantities are unknown (e.g., highway earthmoving). The Owner pays $100 per cubic meter of concrete. The risk is shared: the Contractor risks their efficiency per unit, but the Owner risks the final total quantity required.
  • Cost-Plus (CCDC 3): Used when the scope is entirely uncertain. The Owner agrees to pay all actual costs plus a percentage fee. The danger is that the Contractor has zero incentive to work quickly or efficiently—in fact, a percentage fee actively incentivizes them to inflate costs.

2. The Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) Engine

To fix the fatal flaw of Cost-Plus contracts (where the contractor has no incentive to save money), the industry created the "Target Price" or "GMP" hybrid model.

Visualization Logic: This demonstrates how to engineer financial incentives.

  • The Mechanism: A GMP (Guaranteed Maximum Price) blends the safety of a Fixed-Price contract for the Owner with the transparency of a Cost-Plus contract.
  • Gainshare: If the contractor brings the project in under the target price, they receive a massive bonus (a percentage of the savings). This forces the contractor to hunt for efficiencies.
  • The Hard Cap: If costs explode beyond the Target Price, the GMP acts as a ceiling. The Owner pays nothing above the cap, forcing the Contractor to eat the losses.
