Chapter 2: Regulation of the Professions

1. Elements of Professional Self-Regulation
  • Right to Title: The exclusive right for members to use a particular title (e.g., P.Eng.).
  • Scope of Practice: The exclusive right for members to practice in a particular area or field.
2. Principal Functions of Regulatory Bodies
  • Register members.
  • Regulate the practice of members.
  • Discipline members.
  • Enforce legislation against non-members practicing illegally.
3. Types of Definitions for Scope of Practice
  • Generic Definition: Broadly states the types of tasks that constitute the exclusive scope of practice.
  • Specific Definition: Identifies in detail the individual tasks that make up the exclusive scope.
4. Components of Registration
  • Experience: Generally four years of qualifying experience.
  • Education: Generally a degree from an accredited university.
  • Examinations: A non-technical examination on law and ethics (and sometimes technical exams for non-accredited degrees).
5. Types of Regulatory Proceedings
  • Discipline: Charging a member for incompetence or a breach of the code of ethics.
  • Enforcement: Charging a non-member for using a protected title or practicing the profession illegally.
6. Potential Penalties for Discipline Actions
  • Reprimands.
  • Suspensions.
  • Fines.
  • Termination of licences.
  • Educational requirements.
  • Mentorship requirements.
