2 Types of Co-Ownership of Real Property2 items
▾
1
Joint Tenancy— co-owners hold concurrently; on death, remaining joint tenants automatically inherit (right of survivorship). Default assumption.
2
Tenancy in Common— each holds a stated portion; on death, heirs of the deceased inherit their share (NOT the co-owner)

3 Types of Property Boundary Rights3 items
▾
1
Lateral Support— neighbour cannot undermine support of adjacent land/buildings by digging
2
Riparian Rights— rights to use water on or adjacent to a property
3
Air Rights— extend above property to a reasonable level; prevent neighbours blocking sunlight; used for air space parcels

2 Systems for Registering Real Property2 items
▾
1
Torrens System— western provinces; government-run certificates of title; shows current fee simple holder and all charges; always up to date
2
Registry System— parts of Ontario and eastern provinces; chronological list only; must review historical records to find interests
5
7 Specific Real Property Rights7 items
▾
i
Lease— exclusive right to occupy; generally runs with the land
ii
Mortgage— security for a loan; lender paid from property value on sale; discharged on repayment
iii
Easement / Right of Way— right to use or cross neighbouring land; runs with the land; can be created by historical/repetitive use
iv
Restrictive Covenant— conditions on one property for benefit of neighbour. Negative (refrain) = runs with land. Positive (do something) = does NOT run with land.
v
Licence— right to use property; does NOT run with the land; only between original parties
vi
Profit à Prendre— combo of easement + licence; DOES run with the land; e.g. harvesting trees, removing gravel
vii
Construction Lien— charge on land from unpaid builder; provided by statute in each province; cannot be registered on unpatented land

Copyright — 2 Types of Rights2 items
▾
1
Economic Rights— publication, reproduction, performance, exhibition, translation, dissemination, authorization. Can be assigned.
2
Moral Rights— protect artistic integrity; right to prevent distortions, mutilations, false claims of authorship. Cannot be assigned — only waived.
