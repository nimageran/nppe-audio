5 Duties Owed by A/E/G Professionals5 items · in priority order
▾
1
Duty to the Public— paramount (highest). Safety, health, welfare, fairness, integrity. Cannot be overridden by employer or client pressure.
2
Duty to the Client— second. Faithful agent, confidentiality, avoid conflicts of interest.
3
Duty to the Employer— loyalty, confidentiality, no competing without consent.
4
Duty to the Profession— uphold honour/dignity, report misconduct of fellow members.
5
Duty to Self— lowest priority; typically ranks below all other duties.

2 CFPOA Key Characteristics vs US FCPA2 items
▾
CFPOA (Canada) — Corruption of Foreign Public Officials Act
1
Applies outside Canada— requires "real and substantial" connection to Canada or Canadian nationality (incl. permanent residents)
2
Facilitation payments exception— small payments to expedite routine approvals are currently allowed (Canada is eliminating this)
US FCPA — 2 Key Differences from CFPOA
1
No double jeopardy protection— convicted under CFPOA in Canada? US can still try and convict you for the same conduct
2
No "real and substantial" connection required— even minor US connection (e.g. using US Postal Service) is enough for US jurisdiction
